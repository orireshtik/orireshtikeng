IP Protection & Digital Assets

Supreme Court (Bagatz) Victory for Performers

Reclaiming Rights: The Blank Media Levy Dispute

In Israel, the "Blank Media Levy" law compensating performers for private copying was stuck in 1996, compensating only for obsolete analog tapes. For decades, modern digital devices (Smartphones, USBs, MP3s, Decoders) were ignored, leaving performers uncompensated while the state fund froze at a meager 1.5M ILS (unpaid since 2019).

In 2021, the government attempted to worsen the situation by replacing the statutory compensation with a "support test" (Mivchanei Tmicha), effectively downgrading performers' legal rights to state charity. We refused to accept this.

The Supreme Court Action (Oct 2021)

We filed a decisive petition to the Supreme Court (Bagatz), led by Adv. Barak Bar-Shalom, demanding the cancellation of the "support tests", restoration of statutory compensation, retroactive payment for lost years, and the formation of a professional committee to update the levy for the digital age.

Government Surrender

Modern Device Inclusion

The outcome was unprecedented: The state conceded, agreeing to broadly interpret the law to include modern digital recording devices.

Financial Impact

This legal triumph secures a permanent, statutory, and updated financial engine. Israeli artists are now projected to receive millions of shekels annually as legitimate compensation, rather than relying on arbitrary government grants.

Current Status

The inter-ministerial committee has convened. We are actively accompanying its work alongside top-tier economists and legal experts to ensure the new levy accurately reflects current technological reality.
